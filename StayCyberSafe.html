Be Cybersafe in the Cyberworld

Almost everyone uses the internet these days. You're able to view this website because you have access to the internet. This website is on the web open for anyone to view. Students use the internet for their school projects. Teachers use the internet for more information on a topic, so that the students can be taught more. Buisness men and women use the internet to market their products. Artists put their art on show on the internet too for people to view and their talent to be appreciated. Musicians put their music on the internet and people listen to it with joy. There are so many uses of the internet. We can't imagine our lives without it.

Let me ask you a question- Is the internet really a safe place? There are so many people connected to the internet, as mentioned above. We don't know the people on the internet, we don't know if they are trust-worthy, we don't know if they're safe to talk with.

You must have had your parents,teachers or elders say "Don't talk with strangers" when you were younger. They said so because we don't know what those strangers may do to you. Same way, we can't trust everyone on the internet.

Along with students,teachers,buisness men and other people accessing the internet, there are hackers,kidnappers,bullies and other shady people accessing the internet as well. In order to prevent any danger towards us or our information, we must stay cybersafe.

Some dangers of the internet :-

  • Cyberbullying is probably the best known out of these. This mostly happens to kids and teens on social media platforms. 90% of teens agree that cyber bullying a problem, and 63% believe this is a serious problem. What’s more, a 2018 survey of children’s online behavior found that approximately 60% of children who use social media have witnessed some form of bullying, and that, for various reasons, most children ignored the behavior.It's a problem all over the world.   • Cyber Predators often stalk children on the internet, taking advantage of their innocence, lack of adult supervision and abusing their trust.This can culminate in children being lured into dangerous personal encounters in real life. These predators lurk on social media and gaming platforms that appeal to children — the same virtual venues where anonymity facilitates cyber bullying.

  • Phishing is what cyber security professionals call the use of emails that try to trick people into clicking on malicious links or attachments. These can be especially difficult for kids to detect because often, the email will appear to be from someone legitimate, like a friend or family member, saying simply, "Hey—thought you might like this!" This can also be done with using messaging apps or text messages—then it's called "smishing".
